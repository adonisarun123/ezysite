## About EzyHelpers

EzyHelpers connects urban households across India with verified domestic professionals — live-in maids, full-time and part-time helpers, cooks, nannies, elderly caregivers, drivers, and patient-care attendants. Most of our placements happen in metros and tier-1 cities (Bangalore, Delhi NCR, Hyderabad, Chennai, Mumbai, Pune, Bareilly), but **the supply for those placements comes overwhelmingly from a handful of source-rich states** — the North East, coastal Andhra and Telangana, interior Tamil Nadu, the tribal belts of Odisha and Jharkhand, and parts of West Bengal, Bihar, UP, and Chhattisgarh.

This is where you come in.

## About the role

A **Field Mobiliser** is EzyHelpers' on-the-ground recruitment officer in a source district. Your job is to find, motivate, and onboard the right candidates from your home region for placement opportunities in our city markets — and to do it the right way: with honest conversations, family consent, clear expectations on pay and conditions, and clean documentation.

You are not a tele-caller. You travel — to villages, panchayats, urban wards, ITIs, training centres, churches and temples, women's self-help groups, NGO partners, *anganwadis*, *jan suvidha kendras*, college and skill-mission alumni networks. You explain what a dignified live-in or full-time job in a metro actually looks like (and what it does not), you screen candidates for fit, and you hand off a complete profile to our central sourcing and verification team.

Done well, this is one of the highest-impact roles in the company. A great Field Mobiliser brings 15–25 placement-ready candidates a month, and changes the income trajectory of every single one of them.

## What you will do (Key Responsibilities)

### 1. Source candidates from your assigned cluster
- Build and run a **monthly mobilisation plan** for your district / cluster — villages, blocks, urban wards, training institutes, SHGs, NGOs, panchayat networks, and faith-based community gatherings.
- Conduct **field visits, group meetings, and 1:1 conversations** with prospective candidates (most often women aged 21–55, but also couples for housekeeping / farmhouse / security roles).
- Partner with local stakeholders — Panchayat Secretaries, ASHAs, *anganwadi* workers, ITI placement cells, JSS (Jan Shikshan Sansthan), DDU-GKY and PMKVY centres, NRLM / SRLM SHG federations, church / *mahila mandal* groups, local agents — to generate a consistent inflow of credible leads.
- Run small **community awareness sessions** about live-in, full-time, and patient-care opportunities in cities; bust myths around safety, food, and treatment; explain salary structure, weekly off, leave policy, and return travel.

### 2. Screen and qualify candidates honestly
- Conduct structured screening: age, marital status, number and age of children, family permission, language(s) spoken, prior work, cooking style, food handling, health, willingness to relocate, expected salary, joining timeline.
- **Pre-qualify against current city demand**: live-in maid, full-time maid, cook (veg / non-veg, regional cuisine), nanny, elderly / patient care, couple housekeeping, farmhouse couple, Japa maid, *etc.*
- Set realistic expectations on salary band, working hours, food, accommodation, off-day, and city culture. **Never over-promise** — under-promise and let the city team close.
- Identify and politely exit candidates who are not a fit (medical issues, dependency at home, unrealistic salary expectations, family unwilling, history of frequent job-switching without cause).

### 3. Build complete, audit-ready candidate profiles
- Capture Aadhaar, PAN (where available), photograph, voter ID / ration card, two reference contacts, current and permanent address with landmark, and live geo-tagged location.
- Conduct a basic verbal **reference check** with the candidate's family head or village-level reference before the file leaves your hands.
- Fill out the EzyHelpers helper profile in our app / CHAP system on the same day — skills, languages, prior work history, salary expectation, joining date, photographs, video introduction, category mapping.
- **No ghost profiles. No copy-paste. No exaggerated skills.** The profile you submit is the one a Bangalore or Delhi family will trust their home to.

### 4. Manage the candidate journey from "yes" to "boarded"
- Coordinate medical fitness checklist, document collection, and any state-level migrant-worker registration (where applicable).
- Handle **family counselling** — most candidates need a household conversation, not just an individual one. Be ready to sit with parents, in-laws, husbands, and elder siblings to address concerns about safety and respect.
- Coordinate **travel logistics** with the central placement team: train / bus booking, station pickup at the destination city, contact handoff to the city team and house family.
- Stay reachable to the candidate for the first 30 days of placement; resolve homesickness, salary clarification, and family communication issues before they become drop-outs.

### 5. Coordinate with central sourcing, sales, and verification
- Receive **daily / weekly demand briefs** from the central sourcing lead — what categories are hot, what salary bands are moving, which cities are placing.
- Push candidate profiles into the funnel within agreed TAT (typically same-day or next-day).
- Coordinate with the verification team for police verification, background checks, and any vendor-led screening.
- Flag risk signals (suspect documents, dependency mismatch, family pressure, agent-routing) before placement.

### 6. Reporting, hygiene, and compliance
- Submit **Daily Work Report (DWR)**: villages / locations visited, meetings held, leads generated, candidates screened, profiles created, profiles confirmed, travel covered.
- Maintain accurate app check-in / check-out, geo-stamped photos, and call logs.
- Follow EzyHelpers' **Code of Conduct** strictly: no commission from candidates, no commission from local agents, no false promises of pay, no recruitment of minors, no recruitment without family consent.

## How we measure success (Key KPIs)

- **Leads generated per month** (target: 80–150 per mobiliser per month, region-dependent).
- **Candidates screened and profile-created** per month (target: 30–50).
- **Profiles confirmed as placement-ready** by central sourcing (target: 15–25 per month).
- **Successful placements** in destination cities (target: 8–15 per month).
- **30-day retention rate** of your placed candidates (target: ≥ 80%).
- **Documentation completeness and accuracy** (target: ≥ 95% audit pass rate; zero ghost profiles).
- **Adherence to Code of Conduct** — zero tolerance on commission-taking, minor recruitment, or family-consent violations.
- **Daily Work Report (DWR) compliance** — submitted by 9 PM every working day.

## A typical week (illustrative)

- **Monday morning:** Review the central demand brief on WhatsApp / app — "Bangalore needs 12 live-in cooks (South Indian veg), 8 elderly caregivers, 4 couple housekeepers; salary band updated."
- **Monday–Wednesday:** Visit 2–3 villages or wards per day. One SHG group meeting in the morning, 4–6 individual / family conversations through the day. Profile creation in the evening.
- **Thursday:** Document collection day — Aadhaar, photographs, references; family counselling for the 4–5 candidates who have said yes; coordinate medical and travel.
- **Friday:** Boarding day — drop off 2 candidates at the railway station; handover call with the Bangalore city team; close their files in CHAP.
- **Saturday:** Cluster review with your Regional Supply Head — what worked, what didn't, where the next week's demand is going.
- **Sunday:** Off (unless a boarding is scheduled).

## Growth path

- **Year 1:** Field Mobiliser — own your district / cluster and prove consistent monthly throughput with strong retention.
- **Year 1.5 – 2:** **Senior Field Mobiliser / Cluster Lead** — manage 3–5 mobilisers across a sub-region; own a state-level supply target.
- **Year 2 – 3:** **Regional Supply Head** — own a full state or zone (e.g. "North East", "Coastal AP-Odisha", "Tamil Nadu"), with a team of 8–15 mobilisers, full state-level P&L for supply, and a seat at the central sourcing leadership table.
- **Year 3+:** Cross-functional moves into central operations, training, partnerships, or city leadership.

We promote from within. Almost every senior person in our supply organisation started at the village level.

## Selection process

1. **Application review** (CV + a short voice note in your local language explaining why you fit this role — instructions in the application form).
2. **Phone interview** in your local language with the Regional Supply Head (30–40 minutes).
3. **One-day field shadow** — you accompany an existing mobiliser, or we accompany you to two of your candidate conversations. We assess your community presence and conversational style.
4. **Reference check** with two professional references.
5. **Offer + onboarding** — 5-day paid training at our Bangalore office (travel + stay covered), including 2 days of city-side field exposure so you know exactly where your candidates will be working.

End-to-end timeline: typically **10–18 days** from application to offer.
