Elderly care is different from general caregiving. It demands patience, an understanding of what dignity means to someone who has lived eight decades, and the ability to earn the trust of a person who may be cautious about accepting help from a stranger.

We do not assign a general caretaker to an elderly patient and hope for the best. We don't just look at experience; we also check how caretakers interact with elderly people. Patience, respect, and a calm attitude are very important in senior care. We try to match seniors with caretakers who are gentle, understanding, and supportive in daily life.

## **How Our Placement Process Works**

For elderly care, the quality of the matching process matters more than the speed. We take time to understand your parent as a person, not just as a patient.

### **Step 1: Free Senior Care Consultation**

We begin with a conversation about the senior's health, condition, daily habits, language preference, comfort level, and what has or has not worked in past care experiences. These details directly shape who we shortlist.

### **Step 2: Senior Caretaker Shortlisting**

We identify two to three caretakers with direct elderly care experience and specifically with your parents' conditions. Language fit and condition compatibility are weighted heavily in this shortlist.

### **Step 3: Interview & Matching**

Where possible, we encourage the elderly person to participate in meeting the shortlisted caretakers. First impressions matter, and we do not rush this step. If none of the options feels right, we continue the search.

### **Step 4: Onboarding & Ongoing Support**

After selection, the caretaker is briefed on your parent's medical history, daily preferences, food habits, and patterns to watch for. We follow up during the first week and remain available for ongoing support throughout the placement.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **How do you find a caretaker my elderly parent will actually accept?**

We start with a long, unhurried conversation about who your parent is — language, food, temperament, what mornings look like, what makes them comfortable. We then shortlist caretakers who have worked with similar profiles, and you decide who feels right after meeting them.

### **Can you place a caretaker for an elderly person with dementia?**

Yes. We weight shortlists toward caretakers who have specifically worked with dementia or Alzheimer's patients — patience, repetition, redirection, and managing sundowning are skills we look for explicitly.

### **Is live-in elderly care available through EzyHelpers?**

Yes — live-in is one of our most common arrangements. The caretaker stays in the home with proper rest hours, weekly leave, and we handle scheduling so there's continuous coverage.

### **What if my parent refuses to accept a caretaker?**

It's more common than people think. We've found that easing in slowly — short visits first, daytime only, building rapport — works better than a full live-in start. We'll talk through approaches with you.

### **Can I request a female caretaker for my mother?**

Of course. Most placements for elderly women are with female caretakers, and we honour any preference around gender, language, religion, or food without question.

### **Are there fixed contracts for elderly care arrangements?**

Arrangements are flexible. We have monthly placements, longer-term ones, and short relief covers. There's no penalty if you need to end a placement early because the situation changed.

### **How quickly can you arrange a senior caretaker in Bangalore?**

For standard requirements, we can often arrange a meeting within 24–48 hours. For specific matches (rare conditions, particular language, niche experience), we take a little longer to get the fit right.
