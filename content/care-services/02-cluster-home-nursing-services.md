## **How Our Placement Process Works**

Home nurse placement requires a more detailed medical matching process than general caregiving. We take the additional steps this demands seriously.

### **Step 1: Free Nursing Consultation**

We discuss your patient's diagnosis, treatment history, discharge instructions, and the specific nursing procedures the treating physician has prescribed for home care. The more details we receive, the more accurate our nurse matching becomes. This medical briefing determines the type of nurse we shortlist.

### **Step 2: Nurse Shortlisting**

We identify nurses whose qualifications and medical experience match your patient's condition. A patient coming home after cardiac bypass is matched with a nurse with post-cardiac experience, not a general nurse. The shortlist is small and focused, usually two to three nurses.

### **Step 3: Interview & Matching**

We share detailed nurse profiles with their qualifications, experience, and past work details. You can speak with the shortlisted nurses before finalising your selection. For complex cases, this discussion also helps you understand the care approach and how the nurse will manage the patient's needs.

### **Step 4: Onboarding & Ongoing Support**

Once selected, the nurse is briefed on the patient's condition, care routine, and family preferences. If a doctor is involved, we help ensure smooth coordination between the nurse and the medical team for better care continuity. We follow up after the first week and remain available for any support needed.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **What nursing qualifications do EzyHelpers' home nurses hold?**

Our home nurses typically hold GNM, B.Sc Nursing, or equivalent recognised nursing qualifications. We check and verify all certificates and registration before placement. For specific tasks like IV care, catheter care, or wound dressing, we additionally verify that the nurse is trained and experienced in those procedures.

### **Can a home nurse give injections or manage an IV at home?**

Yes, trained nurses can give injections and handle IV therapy at home. This is done only when the doctor has prescribed it, and only by nurses who are skilled and experienced in these procedures.

### **What is the difference between a home nurse and a caretaker?**

A home nurse is medically trained and handles health-related tasks like administering medicines, monitoring vitals, dressing wounds, and supporting treatment. A [caretaker](/care-services/caretaker-services-bangalore/) helps with daily activities like bathing, feeding, mobility, and companionship. Depending on the patient's needs, you can choose one or both — many families combine both for complete care.

### **Can a home nurse and a caretaker both be placed for the same patient?**

Yes. This is a common arrangement. The nurse manages medical tasks like medications and monitoring, while the caretaker supports daily needs like hygiene, feeding, and mobility. This ensures complete care and reduces stress for the family. We coordinate both placements and ensure smooth handover.

### **Can I get a home nurse for post-surgical wound care specifically?**

Yes. Post-surgical wound care is one of the most common home nursing services. The nurse handles dressing changes per the doctor's advice, checks for infection signs, keeps records, and informs the family or doctor if any issues are noticed.

### **Do you offer live-in home nurses for patients with intensive medical needs?**

Yes. Live-in home nurses are available for patients who need continuous care and monitoring. This is helpful after major surgery, hospital discharge, or for serious health conditions where 24-hour support and regular health observation are required at home.

### **Can home nursing services be arranged for night shifts only?**

Yes. Night nursing is available for patients who need monitoring, medication, or assistance during nighttime hours. The nurse is matched based on the same care requirements as a day-shift placement.

### **Is there coordination with the treating doctor's instructions?**

Yes. Every nurse follows the doctor's instructions before starting care. If any health issue is observed, the nurse informs the family and advises further medical attention when required. For patients under active medical supervision, we facilitate structured communication between the nurse and the doctor's team.

### **How quickly can you arrange a home nurse in Bangalore?**

In most cases, we can arrange a nurse within the same day or the next day, depending on your requirement and location. For urgent situations like hospital discharge, we prioritise faster placement from our available nurses.

### **What if the nurse is not a good fit for the patient?**

If you are not comfortable with the assigned nurse, you can request a replacement. We understand that comfort and trust are important, especially in home care, so we support smooth replacement when needed.
