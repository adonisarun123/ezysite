## **How Our Placement Process Works**

For home healthcare placements, our process includes extra steps to ensure both medical and daily-care professionals match your patient's situation.

### **Step 1: Free Consultation**

We speak with you to understand the patient's diagnosis, current care instructions, what kind of monitoring is required, and what daily-care support is needed. This helps us recommend the right combination of nurse and caretaker (or whether one is sufficient).

### **Step 2: Coordinated Shortlisting**

Based on your needs, we shortlist suitable professionals — both medical and non-medical where required. For example, cardiac patients are matched with nurses experienced in post-cardiac recovery and caretakers familiar with cardiac patient routines.

### **Step 3: Interview & Matching**

We share detailed profiles, including qualifications, relevant experience, and past work details. You can review them, speak with the shortlisted professionals if needed, and choose those you are most comfortable with.

### **Step 4: Onboarding & Ongoing Support**

Once selected, the professionals are properly briefed about the patient's condition, care plan, and daily routine. If the patient is under a doctor's supervision, we help with basic coordination to ensure care is followed correctly. We follow up after the first week and stay available throughout.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **What is the difference between home healthcare and home care?**

Home care primarily covers daily personal support — hygiene, feeding, medication reminders, mobility — without medical procedures. Home healthcare combines this with medical monitoring, wound observation, vital signs documentation, doctor coordination, and where needed, licensed nursing procedures. We offer both and can help you determine which level fits your patient.

### **Do you provide post-surgical home healthcare?**

Yes. Post-surgical home healthcare is one of our most common placements. We match professionals based on the specific surgery type — cardiac, orthopaedic, abdominal, neurological, or other — to ensure the team has directly relevant experience.

### **Can home healthcare include medical nursing procedures?**

Yes. For patients who need licensed nursing procedures at home, such as wound dressing changes, IV management, or catheter care, we place qualified home nurses rather than general caregivers. We verify nursing qualifications as part of the placement process.

### **How do you coordinate with the patient's treating doctor?**

Our professionals are briefed on the treating physician's home care plan before placement. If the physician provides written instructions, we ensure these are followed. For patients under active medical supervision, we facilitate structured communication between the home healthcare team and the treating doctor.

### **Is same-day home healthcare available for hospital discharge?**

In urgent situations like hospital discharge, we aim to arrange support as quickly as possible. If a suitable professional is available, placement may be possible on the same day. Since proper matching is important, timelines can vary, and we recommend reaching out in advance whenever possible.

### **Can home healthcare be arranged on a long-term basis?**

Yes. Home healthcare can be arranged for long-term support based on the patient's condition. This is especially helpful for individuals managing ongoing health issues such as diabetes, heart conditions, kidney problems, or breathing difficulties. Care plans can be continued as long as required and adjusted over time.

### **Can I get a nurse and a caretaker for the same patient?**

Yes — that's exactly what home healthcare is. The nurse handles medical procedures while the caretaker manages daily personal care. We coordinate both placements, ensure smooth handover, and keep communication aligned.
